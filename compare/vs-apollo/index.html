TradeBridge vs Apollo for Home Services Contacts

TradeBridge vs Apollo for contractor contacts. See how license-verified trade data compares to generic B2B prospecting tools.

The quick version

Apollo is a sales engagement platform with a built-in B2B contact database. It's significantly cheaper than ZoomInfo (free tier available, paid plans from $49/month). But for contractor data, it has the same fundamental problem: no license verification, no trade segmentation, and contacts sourced from web scraping rather than state boards.

TradeBridge provides license-verified contractor contacts segmented by trade type. Per-project pricing with no subscription.

Apollo's strength is engagement, not contractor data

Apollo's real value is its sales engagement features: email sequences, call tracking, meeting scheduling. The contact database is a means to that end. For tech sales, the database is good enough. For contractor data, it's not.

Apollo's contractor records come from web scraping and user-contributed data. They don't source from state licensing boards. You can search for "plumbing companies" but you'll get a mix of plumbing supply stores, plumbing fixture manufacturers, and actual plumbing contractors. No way to filter for "licensed residential plumber."

Where TradeBridge adds value

TradeBridge delivers the data. Apollo delivers the engagement tools. They work together. Buy your verified contractor list from TradeBridge, import it into Apollo (or any other outreach tool), and run your campaigns against data you can trust.

This is how most of our customers work. They have an outreach tool they like. They just need better data going into it. TradeBridge provides that data: license-verified, trade-segmented, deliverability-tested.

Cost math

Apollo's paid plans start at $49/month ($588/year). That gets you access to their database with export limits. For heavy usage, you'll pay $99-$199/month. But you're still working with unverified contractor data that needs manual cleaning.

TradeBridge charges per contact with no subscription. A targeted list of 2,000 verified electrical contractors in California costs $600-$1,500. You get it once, use it in any tool, and order more when you need it.
