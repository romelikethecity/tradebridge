The Problem

Generic B2B data wasn't built for home services

These tools work for selling SaaS to tech companies. They fall apart the moment you need accurate contractor data.

ZoomInfo tags them as "construction" or "home improvement"

That includes hardware stores, lumber yards, and interior designers. You need the owner of a 6-truck HVAC shop in Phoenix. ZoomInfo can't find him.

Apollo has no license verification

No way to know if a contractor is actually licensed or if their license expired. You could be contacting unlicensed operators or companies that closed last year.

No trade segmentation

Generic B2B tools can't distinguish HVAC from plumbing from electrical. If you're selling heat pump equipment, you need HVAC contractors, not a list of "home services companies."

Enterprise pricing for bad data

You're paying $15K-$30K per year for a platform where you still have to manually filter thousands of irrelevant results. The platform fee is just the start of your cost.

The Solution

Purpose-built contractor data, verified against state boards

State board verified

Every contractor cross-checked against their state licensing board. Active license confirmed before delivery.

Built for outreach
